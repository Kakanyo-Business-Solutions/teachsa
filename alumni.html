About Alumni Network

The TEACH South Africa Alumni Programme and Network represent an integral and dynamic component of the organisation's comprehensive approach to education transformation. This initiative is designed to support and engage former TEACH South Ambassadors, creating a vibrant community that fosters ongoing growth, advocacy, and collaboration. Alumni of the programme gain access to a range of benefits and opportunities that make their continued involvement both rewarding and impactful.

TEACH Alumni stay actively engaged, collaborate with like-minded individuals, and continue their journey as leaders in education. This sense of community and the range of benefits and opportunities available ensure that Alumni remain committed advocates for quality education educational equity in South Africa.

Benefits and Opportunities for Alumni:

  • Advocacy for Educational Equity: TEACH Alumni have a unique platform to advocate for educational equity and be the voice of positive change in the South African education landscape. They can actively contribute to shaping policies and practices that address critical challenges and promote inclusive education
  • Professional Development: The TEACH Alumni Network provides opportunities for continuous professional growth. This includes access to further training, workshops, and resources, ensuring that Alumni remain at the forefront of educational practices
  • Mentorship and Support: Former Ambassadors often play a key role in mentoring and supporting new generations of Ambassadors, helping them navigate the challenges of the teaching profession and ensuring a smooth transition into their roles.
  • Networking and Collaboration: TEACH Alumni become part of a vibrant community of educators and leaders who share a common commitment to educational equity. This network offers opportunities for collaboration, idea-sharing, and mutual support.
  • Leadership Opportunities: TEACH Alumni are empowered to take on leadership roles not only within the education sector but in various fields. They are well-equipped with the leadership qualities to make a positive impact beyond the classroom.
  • Community Engagement: TEACH Alumni remain connected with the TEACH South Africa community and have opportunities to engage in community service and outreach, contributing to the well-being of underserved communities
  • Research and Advocacy: TEACH Alumni can participate in research and advocacy efforts to address educational disparities, contributing to evidence-based solutions and policy recommendations.

Alumni can stay connected by:

  • Joining our alumni network closed group page on whatsapp, contact Bridget Mukwevho on 073 324 7182
  • Attend networking events to help current service leaders hone their networking skills.
  • Share any information and opportunities with our current ambassadors.
  • Help us look for sponsors, partners, and funders.
  • Continue to develop yourself and make time to do small acts of kindness throughout your journey.

TEACH South Africa (TEACH) is a non-profit organisation dedicated to improving learner performance and educational outcomes in South Africa. TEACH recruits, trains, and places highly motivated, skilled, and passionate top Science, Technology, English, and Mathematics (STEM) graduates in underperforming schools nationwide for a 2-year cohort.
